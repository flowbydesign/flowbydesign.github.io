California \

Convict Lake / Aug.13

_A lake in the Sherwin Range of the Sierra Nevada. It is known for its fishing and the dramatic mountains that surround the lake. The lake was named after an incident on September 23, 1871, where a group of convicts escaped from prison in Carson City.

Trinity Alps Wilderness / July.13

_The high, granitic and ultramafic peaks of the eastern half of the wilderness area are known as the Trinity Alps. Granite peaks at the core of the area are known as the White Trinities, reddish ultramafic peaks in the southeast are known as the Red Trinities, and the forested mountains in the western half of the wilderness are known as the Green Trinities.

Lassen Volcanic National Park / June.13

_The dominant feature of the park is Lassen Peak, the largest plug dome volcano in the world and the southern-most volcano in the Cascade Range. Lassen Volcanic National Park is one of the few areas in the world where all four types of volcano can be found (plug dome, shield, cinder cone, and strato).

Avenue of Giants / Sept.09

_A scenic highway in the U.S. state of California, running through Humboldt Redwoods State Park. It is an old alignment of U.S. Route 101, and continues to be maintained by the state as State Route 254
